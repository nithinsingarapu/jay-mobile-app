Quick Tips
💡
Thinnest to thickest
Always layer water-based products before oil-based. Serums before creams.
☀️
2 finger-lengths of SPF
That's how much sunscreen you need for your face. Reapply every 2 hours outdoors.
🌙
Retinol = PM only
It degrades in sunlight and makes your skin photosensitive. Always at night.
⏱
Wait between actives
Give vitamin C and other actives 1-2 minutes to absorb before layering.

Skin Cycling
4-night cycleAll skin typesDr. Whitney Bowe
Skin cycling is a strategic rotation of active ingredients across a repeating multi-night cycle. The standard 4-night cycle prevents the cumulative irritation that occurs when potent ingredients are applied nightly.
The 4-night protocol
1
Exfoliation Night
AHA or BHA to remove dead cells and unclog pores
2
Retinoid Night
Retinol or prescription retinoid for cell turnover and collagen
3
Recovery Night
Ceramide moisturizer + hyaluronic acid only. Let skin rest.
4
Recovery Night
Same as Night 3. Barrier-supportive products only. Then repeat.
Key ingredients
Glycolic Acid (AHA)Salicylic Acid (BHA)RetinolCeramidesHyaluronic Acid
Best for
Sensitive skin beginners to retinol
Those experiencing retinol irritation
Anyone wanting structured active rotation
Source
Popularized by board-certified dermatologist Dr. Whitney Bowe. Based on the principle that spacing actives prevents cumulative irritation while maintaining efficacy.

How the Skin Barrier Works
3 min read · Dermatology basics

The stratum corneum — the outermost layer of your epidermis — is your skin's primary barrier. Think of it as a brick wall: corneocytes (dead skin cells) are the bricks, and a lipid matrix of ceramides, cholesterol, and fatty acids is the mortar.

Every skincare routine either strengthens or weakens this wall. Moisturizers with ceramides reinforce the mortar. Retinoids accelerate brick replacement. AHAs dissolve the old mortar to reveal fresher bricks underneath.

When the barrier is compromised — from over-exfoliation, harsh cleansers, or environmental damage — water escapes (transepidermal water loss), irritants penetrate, and inflammation follows. That tight, stinging feeling after washing? That's barrier damage.

The fix?

Stop all actives. Use only a gentle cleanser, a ceramide-dominant moisturizer (3:1:1 ratio of ceramides, cholesterol, and fatty acids), and mineral sunscreen. Your barrier typically recovers in 2-6 weeks.
